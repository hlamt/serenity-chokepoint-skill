# V0.5 Manual Source Digest Demo Conversation

## 1. Scenario

This is a fictional demonstration of how a user and an assistant can turn a short, hypothetical public expression into a bounded Source Digest. No real Serenity text, real company, or real investment conclusion is used.

## 2. User input

**User**

I saw this short community-style note:

> In the hypothetical AI cooling-module chain, a custom microchannel seal may take longer to qualify than nearby hardware, so expansion can be delayed even when assembly capacity is available.

Can you turn it into a Source Digest and tell me what it means?

## 3. Assistant response

**Assistant**

### Source and provenance boundary

- `source_type: user-provided note / hypothetical public expression`
- Attribution is insufficient: no author, publication, URL, publication date, or primary-source provenance is available.
- The **excerpt** is only the quoted sentence above.
- The **paraphrase** is a neutral restatement and must not strengthen the sentence.
- **Translation:** none; the excerpt is already in English.
- **Interpretation:** the sentence raises a possible qualification-delay mechanism.
- **Assistant inference:** such a mechanism could become a candidate chokepoint research question, but the source does not establish that it exists or affects throughput.

## 4. User asks for stronger conclusion

**User**

Can we mark this as a confirmed chokepoint and list winners?

**Assistant**

No. A single fictional, unattributed note cannot establish a confirmed chokepoint or support a list of company winners. Doing so would erase the provenance boundary and convert an `L0` question generator into an unsupported conclusion.

Before confidence could increase, the research would need at least:

- Company disclosure describing the component, capacity, qualification status, and constraints.
- Regulatory filing evidence with traceable sections and dates.
- Market data defining supply, demand, pricing, units, and time period.
- Customer validation that the component affects qualification or system throughput.
- Capacity and lead-time evidence, including yields, expansion plans, and delivery timing.
- Substitution checks covering alternate designs, materials, and suppliers.
- Counterevidence that could explain or disprove the claimed constraint.
- Explicit invalidation conditions, such as rapid qualification, capacity additions, redesign, demand decline, or constraint migration.

Even after those checks, any company discussion would remain a research hypothesis with risks and evidence grades—not investment advice or a claim of guaranteed winners.

## 5. Final safe output

**Assistant**

### Candidate research question

Could qualification time or limited qualified capacity for a specialized seal reduce throughput in the **hypothetical AI infrastructure cooling module supply chain**, despite adequate module-assembly capacity?

### Source Digest summary

A fictional user-provided note suggests a possible qualification-delay mechanism. It has unknown provenance, no URL, no date, no primary evidence, and an `L0` evidence level. The source is not Serenity original text and does not prove a chokepoint.

### Missing checks

- Verify provenance and obtain primary documentation.
- Define the component, system goal, throughput, geography, customer group, and time horizon.
- Test capacity, yield, lead time, qualification duration, customer dependence, pricing power, and value capture.
- Check alternate suppliers, substitute materials, redesign options, and capacity expansion.
- Seek customer validation, independent market data, counterevidence, and invalidation conditions.

### Next research step

Create a verification plan for **candidate component supplier type A** that identifies the exact hypothetical disclosures, customer evidence, capacity data, and substitution tests required to confirm or reject the mechanism.
