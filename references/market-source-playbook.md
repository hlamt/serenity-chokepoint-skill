# Market Source Playbook

Use public sources that can be dated and revisited. Record document title, issuer or publisher, publication date, accessed date when relevant, URL or filing identifier, and the exact claim supported.

| Source type | Useful for | Common limitations |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 年报 / 季报 / 公告 | Revenue mix, capacity, capex, customers, risks, guidance | Reporting lag, aggregation, management framing |
| 招股书 | Industry structure, suppliers, customers, technology, capacity | Historical snapshot, issuer incentives |
| 互动平台 | Management clarifications and investor questions | Informal wording, selective answers, weak verification |
| 招投标 | Demand timing, specifications, participants, pricing clues | Award changes, incomplete private-market coverage |
| 专利 | Technical direction, assignees, inventors, citations | Patent count does not equal commercialization |
| 供应链调研 | Lead time, allocation, substitution, qualification | Sampling bias, access bias, confidentiality risk |
| 监管文件 | Compliance, approvals, ownership, enforcement | Jurisdiction and timing differences |
| 行业报告 | Market map, terminology, historical estimates | Method opacity, paywall excerpts, circular sourcing |
| 价格 / 产能 / 交期数据 | Scarcity, utilization, expansion, normalization | Product mismatch, channel inventory, inconsistent units |

## Collection Order

1. Start with regulatory and company primary documents.
2. Add operational indicators such as capacity, yield, lead time, qualification, orders, and realized pricing.
3. Use industry reports to widen the map, then trace important claims back to original sources.
4. Add independent counterevidence and alternative explanations.
5. Date-stamp the conclusion and define what future data would change it.
