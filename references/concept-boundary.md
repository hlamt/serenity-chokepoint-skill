# Concept Boundary

Use these definitions to avoid collapsing different problems into one label.

| Term | Working definition | Research test |
| --- | --- | --- |
| supply-chain chokepoint | A hard-to-replace node whose scarcity or control can materially limit value-chain output and enable value capture. | Does it affect system throughput, resist substitution, and retain bargaining power? |
| bottleneck | A general-language label for a limiting or slow point. It is ambiguous without a declared system boundary. | Which of the more precise terms below is intended? |
| TOC-style constraint | The factor that most limits a defined system from achieving its goal at a given time. | Are system goal, boundary, and throughput explicitly defined? |
| CCR | A capacity-constrained resource that governs or may govern flow and therefore requires careful scheduling and subordination. | Does variation or overload at this resource control downstream throughput? |
| supply shortage | Demand temporarily exceeds available supply. It may clear through inventory, imports, substitution, or rapid expansion. | Is scarcity persistent after near-term supply responses? |
| market hype | Attention, narrative, or valuation momentum that may not correspond to physical constraints or economic capture. | Is there primary evidence beyond commentary and price action? |
| local bottleneck | A constrained step inside one plant, process, or firm that may not constrain the whole value chain. | Does relieving it increase system-level throughput? |
| “卡脖子”叙事 | A broad strategic-autonomy narrative about dependence or vulnerability. | Does it identify a current, throughput-limiting, economically capturable chokepoint? |

## Usage Rule

Default to “供应链卡点” for the project's target phenomenon. Use “瓶颈” only with a qualifier that identifies supply-chain chokepoint, local bottleneck, or TOC-style constraint.

Do not treat strategic importance alone as proof of scarcity, throughput impact, or investment value capture.
