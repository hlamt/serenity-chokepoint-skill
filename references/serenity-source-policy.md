# Serenity Source Policy

Serenity is treated as a public framework source, not as an investment signal oracle. Serenity public expression may help frame supply-chain chokepoint research, but it does not replace industry-chain evidence, regulatory filings, company disclosure, or market data.

## Core rules

- **Separate public expression from interpretation**: label Serenity original public expression separately from user interpretation, community interpretation, translation, and assistant inference.
- **Use attribution**: every Serenity public expression must include attribution to the supplied or cited source when available.
- **Prefer paraphrase**: use paraphrase with attribution when it preserves meaning and reduces unnecessary copying.
- **Use short quotation only when necessary**: use a short quotation for disputed wording, terminology, or exact framing, and keep the quotation boundary visible.
- **No endorsement claim**: do not claim this Skill, repository, output, or user research has Serenity's official endorsement, authorization, approval, or review.
- **Do not impersonate**: do not write as Serenity, simulate Serenity, or imply that the assistant can produce Serenity's current or private opinion.
- **No private-intent inference**: do not infer private intent, portfolio position, undisclosed view, or future action from public wording.
- **Do not elevate community interpretation**: translated community interpretations are not original Serenity claims and must be labeled as secondary interpretation.
- **No investment advice**: Serenity expression must not be converted into investment advice, buy/sell instructions, return promises, or real company conclusions.

## Practical labeling

Use labels such as:

- `Serenity public expression`: short original expression or attributed paraphrase.
- `Community interpretation`: secondary summary, translation, or commentary.
- `Supply-chain evidence`: disclosure, filing, transcript, report, data, or news item.
- `Assistant inference`: research hypothesis, risk list, tracking indicator, or invalidation condition.

## If source provenance is unclear

If provenance is unclear, treat the item as community interpretation or user-provided note, reduce confidence, and ask for traceable public attribution before treating it as Serenity public expression.
