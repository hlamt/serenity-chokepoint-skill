# Chokepoint Theory Core｜V1.3A

## 一、Definition

Chokepoint Theory is a bottom-up supply-chain reverse-engineering method for identifying narrow, physically constrained, hard-to-replicate nodes in a system where downstream buildout depends on limited allocation, capacity, materials, process know-how, or supplier concentration.

Chokepoint Theory 是一种自下而上的供应链逆向工程方法，用来识别系统中狭窄、物理受限、难复制、被少数供应方控制、且下游扩张必须依赖其产能或分配权的关键节点。

This definition is a method-core synthesis, not a validated claim that any specific company is a chokepoint, beneficiary, or investment candidate.

## 二、Core Question

In this system, who or what is the silent, irreplaceable physical switch?

在这个系统里，谁是那个沉默但不可替代的物理开关？

The question should be answered with evidence levels, source labels, and invalidation checks. It must not be answered as a buy list.

## 三、Operational Process

1. Define the downstream buildout.
2. Reverse-engineer the supply chain.
3. Identify physical constraints.
4. Identify capacity and concentration.
5. Locate allocation control.
6. Assess hyperscaler / downstream game theory.
7. Separate supplier participation from control-point power.
8. Test firm value capture.
9. Separately test shareholder capture.
10. Track validation signals.
11. Track invalidation signals.
12. Reassess bottleneck migration over time.

Use the process as a research workflow. A company appearing in the chain is only a starting point; it does not prove control, economics, shareholder capture, or investment merit.

## 四、Key Distinctions

| Concept | Meaning | Not Enough By Itself |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Dependency | Downstream needs it | Does not prove bottleneck |
| Bottleneck | Slows throughput | Does not prove durable control |
| Chokepoint | Narrow, hard-to-replicate control point | Must show concentration and allocation power |
| TOC constraint | Current system-level throughput limiter | Must be proven for a defined system |
| Firm value capture | Company can capture economics | Does not prove shareholder capture |
| Shareholder capture | Public holders benefit after dilution, timing, valuation, governance | Requires separate proof |

Additional guardrail: a chokepoint candidate can be important without being the current TOC-style active constraint. A TOC-style constraint requires a defined system boundary, system goal, and throughput test.

## 五、Representative Case Patterns

These are case patterns, not investment recommendations:

| Case pattern | Method relevance | Boundary note |
| --- | --- | --- |
| AXTI / InP substrate | Upstream substrate and materials concentration pattern | Must test concentration, capacity, customer validation, value capture, and China/geopolitical risk |
| SIVE / CW laser / ELS | Next-generation photonics light-source allocation pattern | Must test qualification, foundry allocation, customer proof, design-out risk, and materiality |
| AAOI / vertical integration | Laser-to-transceiver integration pattern | Integration can improve economics, but may still face customer bargaining and cycle risk |
| POET / optical interposer | Mid-chain integration platform pattern | Platform relevance requires customer adoption and volume proof |
| RPI / edge hardware demand sensitivity | Small-market-cap demand convexity pattern | Demand sensitivity is not the same as a physical chokepoint |
| VLN / code collision | Information-structure mispricing pattern, not pure physical chokepoint | Treat as information-structure pattern rather than supply-chain control point |
| NBIS / mechanical panic | Non-fundamental forced-selling / valuation-window pattern, not pure supply-chain chokepoint | Treat as market-structure pattern rather than physical chokepoint |

Case patterns should help users test a hypothesis. They must not be presented as proof that the named company is currently attractive, cheap, or buyable.

## 六、Validation Signals

Useful validation signals include:

1. Earnings call shortage language
2. LTA / prepayment / capacity reservation
3. Customer qualification or design win
4. OSINT customer reference
5. Foundry / epiwafer / supplier dependency reference
6. Capex bottleneck disclosure
7. Lead-time expansion
8. Pricing power / ASP increase
9. M&A or vertical integration interest
10. Institutional rotation into the layer

Validation signals increase confidence only when they are source-labeled, dated, cross-checked, and tied to system throughput. One signal is not enough to prove a chokepoint.

## 七、Invalidation Signals

Useful invalidation signals include:

1. Design-out risk
2. Alternative technology path
3. Capacity expansion by competitors
4. Customer qualification failure
5. Materiality too small to affect revenue
6. Dilution or capital structure leakage
7. Weak shareholder capture
8. Geopolitical blockage
9. Demand timing miss
10. Thesis becomes fully priced in

Invalidation signals should be stated before conclusions become too strong. The answer should explain what would narrow, downgrade, or reject the hypothesis.

## 八、User-Facing Output Discipline

Use internal rules silently.

Answer with direct reasoning.

Do not cite version numbers, registry IDs, or framework rules in user-facing research answers unless the user explicitly asks about internal Skill design.

When listing companies, frame them as research candidates, not recommendations.

Do not rank companies by investment attractiveness. Group them by value-chain layer, control-point hypothesis, evidence strength, validation needs, firm-capture risk, and shareholder-capture risk.

Prefer language such as:

- This is a research hypothesis, not an investment recommendation.
- This company participates in a relevant layer, but that does not prove control-point power.
- Firm value capture and shareholder capture need separate evidence.
- The hypothesis would weaken if substitution, capacity expansion, design-out, or valuation evidence moves against it.

## 十、Non-Investment Boundary

This Skill may produce research hypotheses and candidate company universes.

It must not produce buy/sell/hold recommendations, price targets, position sizing, or direct investment conclusions.

It may redirect a direct stock-picking request into:

- supply-chain mapping
- candidate research universe
- validation checklist
- invalidation checklist
- firm value-capture review
- shareholder-capture review
- risk and monitoring plan
